Ian Rios

Computer Scientist, Audio DSP Researcher, and Electronic Musician

My name is Ian. I'm a Senior Computer Science and Computer Music student at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. I experiment with music and sound in various programming aspects every single day. Most of my free time is devoted to designing audio and MIDI plugins, creating sound via synthesizers, composing music for various projects, and educating myself about acoustics and new concepts within the 21st century computer music realm.

This past summer, I worked for My Music Machines Inc., a company specializing in creating musical instruments that are accessible to the disabled. I primarily worked in Max for Live and Javascript, and had experience translating my software work into hardware.

In addition to my work experience, I also create VST plugins and Max for Live Devices on my own. Three of my projects are already completed: RAAPT, a Rhythmic Automated Audio Processing Templater; Grandulator, a Max device for ensemble granular performances; and Borilliant, which is a full stack open source web project that I worked on to help me and my fellow classmates schedule classes based on student inputed class difficulty and professor rankings. All of these projects are currently free to download and use.
