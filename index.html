Ian Rios

Computer Scientist, Audio DSP Researcher, and Electronic Musician

My name is Ian. I graduated from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in may 2018 with a bachelors in Computer Science and a minor in Computer Music. I experiment with music and sound in various programming aspects every single day. Most of my free time is devoted to designing audio and MIDI plugins, creating sound via synthesizers, composing music for various projects, and educating myself about acoustics and new concepts within the 21st century computer music realm.

I currently work for Awesome Inc as an Assistant Instructor for the Spring 2019 Bootcamp.

This past summer, I got a job as a Technology Apprentice working for Conversant Media, a multimedia advertisement agency. Starting in June, I worked as a systems administrator working with servers and learning how to work with large databases. I created a program that allowed for power data of ad stacks (grouped server racks) to be viewed neatly in an excel sheet that automatically averaged and displayed thousands of values showing problem stacks that needed to be re-racked. I then moved on to the systems engineering team, which I enjoyed much more and was able to use my skills much more. Here, I learned what it is like to work on a team with JIRA and participated in daily scrum meetings. I worked on a project that compared the entries in two databases, and generated several csv files that would then be emailed to the process engineering team, all automated in python.

The summer of 2017, I worked for My Music Machines Inc., a company specializing in creating musical instruments that are accessible to the disabled. I primarily worked in Max for Live and JavaScript, and had experience translating my software work into hardware.

In addition to my work experience, I also create VST plugins and Max for Live Devices on my own as well as new code experiments with ruby and python in and out of class for my personal enjoyment. Six of my projects are already completed: RAAPT, a Rhythmic Automated Audio Processing Templater; Grandulator, a Max device for ensemble granular performances; Barely Enough Ingredients, a webapp for storing and getting food recommendations; Borilliant, which is a full stack open source web project that I worked on to help me and my fellow classmates schedule classes based on student inputted class difficulty and professor rankings; several IBM Bluemix webapp concepts with Watson; and the start of some Max for Live Devices with MIDI Networking. All of these projects are currently free to download and use.
